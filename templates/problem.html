Problem Statement

Insurance companies process a large volume of claims with highly variable costs. While most claims are relatively small, a small fraction of extreme claims contributes disproportionately to total losses. Accurately modeling this tail behavior is therefore critical for effective risk management.

Why Claim Severity Modeling Matters

Claim severity estimation plays a central role in pricing, reserving, and capital planning. Underestimating severe claims can lead to insufficient reserves and unexpected financial stress, while overestimation results in inefficient capital usage.

Traditional mean-based regression models fail to capture the asymmetric and heavy-tailed nature of insurance losses. From a business perspective, the cost of underpredicting extreme claims is far greater than overpredicting small ones.

Dataset Description

This project uses the Allstate Claims Severity dataset, originally released as a Kaggle competition. Each row represents an individual insurance claim with anonymized policy and claim attributes.

Key Challenges

  • Highly skewed loss distribution with a long right tail
  • Presence of extreme outliers with large financial impact
  • High-dimensional feature space dominated by categorical variables
  • Limited interpretability due to anonymized features

Why Quantile Regression

To address these challenges, this project adopts a quantile regression framework. Instead of predicting the expected claim cost, the model directly estimates conditional quantiles such as the 90th and 95th percentiles.

This approach enables explicit control over tail risk and provides actionable thresholds for stress testing, pricing strategies, and operational decision-making.

Project Objective

The objective is to build a robust quantile regression model that delivers reliable and well-calibrated predictions in the upper tail of the claim severity distribution.
